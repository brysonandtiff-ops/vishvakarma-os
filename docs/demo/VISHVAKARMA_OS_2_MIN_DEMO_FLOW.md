# Vishvakarma.OS — 2-Minute Demo Flow

**Purpose:** Give reviewers, investors, testers, and potential buyers a fast, honest walkthrough of the strongest Vishvakarma.OS story.

**Core story:** Landing → Projects demo card → Editor → 2D/3D → AI Copilot proof flow → Export preview.

**Target length:** 120 seconds.

**Audience:** Non-technical reviewer, investor, builder, designer, homeowner, or acquisition evaluator.

**Truth rule:** Do not claim certified building approval, engineering validation, or fixed-price quoting. Use: _decision-support_, _concept estimate_, _prototype/pilot_, and _export preview_.

---

## Demo setup checklist

Before recording:

1. Open the deployed app in a clean browser profile or incognito window.
2. Use iPad landscape or desktop 1440×900+.
3. Confirm `/projects` shows demo cards when no saved projects exist.
4. Confirm a demo card opens the editor with a real blueprint.
5. Confirm 3D view is visible or can be toggled on.
6. Confirm Architecture Copilot can reach the deliverables view with the proof flow visible.
7. Confirm export preview / permit package / compliance PDF controls are visible.
8. Keep personal keys, dashboards, and private Supabase/Vercel/Stripe screens out of the recording.

## Exact narration script

### 0:00–0:12 — Landing

> This is Vishvakarma.OS, a browser-native architecture workstation built for turning early house ideas into a guided 2D and 3D design workflow.

### 0:12–0:25 — Projects demo path

> Instead of starting on a blank app, reviewers can open ready demo blueprints from the Projects page. These are local demo fixtures, so they do not write to Supabase until saved.

### 0:25–0:42 — Open demo blueprint

> I’ll open a ready blueprint so we can immediately review a real floor plan rather than explaining the product from a static mockup.

### 0:42–1:05 — Editor 2D

> Inside the editor, users can inspect walls, openings, rooms, dimensions, tools, project actions, and workspace controls. The goal is to make floor-plan review visual, fast, and understandable.

### 1:05–1:22 — 3D preview

> The same plan can be reviewed in 3D, helping homeowners, families, builders, or designers understand the space before money is spent on final drafting or construction decisions.

### 1:22–1:45 — AI Copilot proof flow

> The Architecture Copilot proof flow shows the full AI story: the brief is captured, inputs are reviewed, a concept is generated, an AUD estimate is shown, compliance notes are marked as decision-support, and export readiness is summarised.

### 1:45–1:58 — Export preview

> From here, the design can move toward editor handoff, Compliance PDF, and permit-package style export preview. It is not a certified approval system yet — it is concept and decision-support software.

### 1:58–2:00 — Close

> Vishvakarma.OS helps people see, test, and explain building ideas before they become expensive.

## Recording rules

Keep it clean:

- No secrets, keys, dashboards, private emails, or browser history.
- No unsupported claims.
- No “certified approval” language.
- No “replaces architects/engineers” language.
- No over-explaining features that are still preview/prototype.

Use these phrases:

- “browser-native architecture workstation”
- “concept design and review”
- “decision-support compliance notes”
- “AUD estimate”
- “export preview”
- “pilot-ready product surface”

Avoid these phrases:

- “guaranteed council approval”
- “engineering certified”
- “complete CAD/BIM replacement”
- “fixed construction quote”
- “official world record”
